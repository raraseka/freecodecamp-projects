Dr. Norman Borlaug's biography

Norman Borlaug was born on March 25, 1914, in Cresco, Iowa, United States. He was an American scientist and humanitarian whose breakthroughs in wheat breeding transformed global agriculture. He created high-yield, disease-resistant crops that helped countries on the brink of famine dramatically boost food production.

Fun Fact:

  • He’s known as “the man who saved a billion lives.”
  • He is one of only seven people to receive the Nobel Peace Prize, the Presidential Medal of Freedom, and the Congressional Gold Medal.
  • His wheat helped countries like India and Pakistan avoid massive famines and achieve record-breaking harvests.
